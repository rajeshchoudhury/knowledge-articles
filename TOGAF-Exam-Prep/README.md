## What Is TOGAF?

TOGAF — **The Open Group Architecture Framework** — is the most widely adopted enterprise architecture framework in the world. Originally developed in 1995, TOGAF provides a systematic approach to designing, planning, implementing, and governing enterprise information technology architecture.

Key facts:

- Maintained by **The Open Group**, a vendor-neutral technology consortium
- Used by over **80% of the world's leading enterprises**
- Now in its **10th edition** (released 2022), restructured as a modular standard
- Provides a common vocabulary, recommended standards, and a method (the ADM) for developing architecture

TOGAF 10 is organized as **the TOGAF Standard** — a collection of six documents rather than a single monolithic specification, making it more modular and easier to maintain.

## Exam Structure

### Part 1: TOGAF Foundation

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Tests knowledge and comprehension of TOGAF terminology, structure, and core concepts |
| **Format** | 40 multiple-choice questions (single correct answer) |
| **Duration** | 60 minutes |
| **Pass Mark** | 60% (24 out of 40 correct) |
| **Delivery** | Proctored exam — online or at a Pearson VUE test center |
| **Open Book** | No |
| **Prerequisite** | None |

**What Part 1 covers:**
- Basic concepts of enterprise architecture and TOGAF
- Core concepts of the Architecture Development Method (ADM)
- ADM Guidelines and Techniques
- Architecture Content Framework
- Enterprise Continuum and tools
- TOGAF Reference Models (TRM, III-RM)
- Architecture Capability Framework
- Architecture Governance

### Part 2: TOGAF Certified

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Tests ability to analyze and apply TOGAF knowledge to real-world scenarios |
| **Format** | 8 scenario-based questions, each with a graduated scoring model |
| **Duration** | 90 minutes |
| **Pass Mark** | 60% (24 out of 40 possible points) |
| **Delivery** | Proctored exam — online or at a Pearson VUE test center |
| **Open Book** | Yes — the official TOGAF documentation is provided during the exam |
| **Prerequisite** | Part 1 Foundation must be passed first (or taken as a combined exam) |

**Scoring for Part 2:**
Each question has 4 answer options scored as follows:
- **Best answer**: 5 points
- **Second best**: 3 points
- **Third best**: 1 point
- **Worst/Distractor**: 0 points

Maximum score: 8 × 5 = 40 points. Pass mark: 24 points (60%).

### Combined Exam Option

You can take Part 1 and Part 2 as a single combined sitting. The combined exam runs for 150 minutes total and you must pass each part independently.

## Exam Day Tips

### Before the Exam
1. **Get a good night's sleep** — cognitive performance drops dramatically with fatigue
2. **Arrive early** (or log in 30 minutes early for online proctoring)
3. **Bring valid government-issued photo ID**
4. **Know the test center rules** — no phones, notes, or smartwatches in the exam room
5. **For online exams**: ensure a clean desk, stable internet, and working webcam/microphone

### During the Exam — Part 1
1. **Read each question twice** before selecting an answer
2. **Watch for qualifiers**: "always," "never," "best," "most likely," "primary"
3. **Eliminate obviously wrong answers** first — you usually can remove 1-2 immediately
4. **Don't overthink** — the Foundation exam tests knowledge, not deep analysis
5. **Flag and return** — if unsure, flag the question and come back
6. **Time management**: aim for ~90 seconds per question, leaving 10 minutes for review

### During the Exam — Part 2
1. **Read the entire scenario carefully** — the context matters enormously
2. **Rank all four options** in your mind before selecting — you get partial credit
3. **Think like TOGAF** — what does the standard recommend, not what you would do in practice
4. **Use the open-book reference** strategically — know where to find key information quickly
5. **Apply the ADM** — most scenarios map to a specific ADM phase; identify it first
6. **Time management**: ~11 minutes per question

### General Strategies
- The exam tests **TOGAF knowledge**, not general architecture knowledge
- When in doubt, choose the answer most aligned with the **TOGAF standard**
- **Part 2 is not pass/fail per question** — partial credit means a "second best" answer still earns points
- Practice under timed conditions before exam day

## How to Use These Materials

### Recommended Approach

1. **Start with the Study Plan** — Follow the [12-Week Study Plan](./STUDY-PLAN.md) for a structured approach
2. **Read each module sequentially** — They build on each other in a logical order
3. **Complete review questions** — Every module ends with exam-style questions
4. **Use flashcards daily** — Spaced repetition is critical for retaining terminology
5. **Take practice tests under exam conditions** — Time yourself, no notes (for Part 1)
6. **Review wrong answers thoroughly** — Understand *why* the correct answer is correct

### Study Strategies

#### For Visual Learners
- Focus on the reference diagrams and redraw them from memory
- Create mind maps linking ADM phases to their inputs/outputs
- Use color-coding in your notes (one color per ADM phase)

#### For Reading/Writing Learners
- Take detailed notes on each module
- Write summaries of each ADM phase in your own words
- Create comparison tables (e.g., deliverables vs. artifacts vs. building blocks)

#### For Kinesthetic/Practical Learners
- Apply TOGAF concepts to a real or fictional project as you study
- Walk through the ADM phases using your current organization as an example
- Teach concepts to a colleague

#### For Auditory Learners
- Read key definitions aloud
- Record yourself explaining ADM phases and listen during commutes
- Form a study group and discuss scenarios

## Exam Registration

### How to Register

1. Visit [The Open Group certification page](https://www.opengroup.org/togaf)
2. Select your exam option:
   - Part 1 only
   - Part 2 only (requires Part 1 pass)
   - Combined Part 1 + Part 2
3. Purchase your exam voucher
4. Schedule through **Pearson VUE** — choose a test center or online proctoring
5. Complete the exam within the voucher validity period (typically 12 months)

### Exam Costs (approximate)

| Exam | Cost (USD) |
|---|---|
| Part 1 Foundation only | ~$395 |
| Part 2 Certified only | ~$395 |
| Combined (Part 1 + Part 2) | ~$695 |

*Prices may vary by region and are subject to change. Check The Open Group website for current pricing.*

### Certification Maintenance

TOGAF certification does not expire, but The Open Group offers a **Certification Maintenance Program** through continuing education and professional development to keep your credential current.
