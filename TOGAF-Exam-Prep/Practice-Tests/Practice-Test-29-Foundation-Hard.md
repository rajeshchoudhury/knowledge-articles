## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** a) Phase E identifies what needs to change; Phase F determines the order in which it changes
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Phase E (Opportunities & Solutions) identifies the major work packages, groups them into projects, and defines Transition Architectures — essentially determining WHAT implementation activities are needed. Phase F (Migration Planning) takes those work packages and creates a prioritized, sequenced Implementation and Migration Plan — determining the ORDER and timeline for execution. Option b) incorrectly assigns the Architecture Vision to Phase E. Option c) confuses Phase E with Phases B-D (which develop the Target Architecture). Option d) is incorrect — neither phase is optional.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** b) ABBs define architecture-level specifications and requirements; SBBs are specific implementations that fulfill those specifications
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The fundamental distinction is that ABBs are abstract, specification-level components that define WHAT is needed (functionality, interfaces, constraints) without specifying HOW, while SBBs are concrete, implementation-level components that specify HOW the ABB requirements are met (specific products, technologies, configurations). Option a) is incorrect — both can be reusable. Option c) incorrectly assigns definition responsibility. Option d) incorrectly assigns phases. ABBs are the architectural "contracts" that SBBs must fulfill.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** a) A View is what you see (the content); a Viewpoint is the perspective from which you see it (the convention for constructing and interpreting the view)
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** A Viewpoint defines the conventions (notation, modeling techniques, analytical methods) for constructing and interpreting a View. A View is the actual representation of the architecture addressing specific stakeholder concerns, created using those conventions. Think of a Viewpoint as the "camera angle and lens settings" and the View as the "photograph" taken using those settings. Option b) incorrectly assigns creators. Option c) is about scope, not the actual distinction. Option d) is incorrect — they are distinct concepts.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** b) The Architecture Vision describes WHAT the architecture should look like; the Statement of Architecture Work defines the scope, approach, and schedule for HOW the architecture work will be done
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Architecture Vision is a high-level description of the target architecture and the business value it will deliver. The Statement of Architecture Work is a project-management-oriented document that defines the scope, constraints, approach, schedule, and resources for the architecture development effort. Option a) is wrong — they are different documents. Option c) incorrectly places the Architecture Vision in Phase B. Option d) incorrectly characterizes them — both contain business and technical elements. They are complementary outputs of Phase A.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** b) Deliverables are contractually specified work products reviewed and signed off; Artifacts are granular work products (catalogs, matrices, diagrams) contained within deliverables
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Deliverables are formal, contractually bound work products that go through review and approval processes. They contain one or more Artifacts. Artifacts are the granular architectural content — catalogs listing entities, matrices showing relationships, and diagrams providing visual representations. Option a) reverses the formality. Option c) incorrectly limits the phases. Option d) is factually wrong — both can contain text and visuals. The containment relationship (Deliverables contain Artifacts) is the key structural distinction.

**Correct Answer:** a) A Strategic Architecture covers the entire enterprise at a high level over a long timeframe; a Segment Architecture covers a specific business area or organizational unit in more detail
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** In the Architecture Landscape, Strategic Architecture provides the overarching enterprise-wide view typically covering 3-5+ years, while Segment Architecture focuses on a specific business segment, division, or organizational unit with more detail. Below that is Capability Architecture, which focuses on specific capabilities or projects. Option b) incorrectly assigns domain limitations. Option c) describes who creates them, not what they are. Option d) is incorrect — they coexist at different levels.

Architecture Principles must support and align with Business Principles but are more specific to architecture. Option a) oversimplifies the relationship.
