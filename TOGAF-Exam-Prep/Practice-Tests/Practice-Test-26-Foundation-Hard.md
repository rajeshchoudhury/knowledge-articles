## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** b) Phase B: Business Architecture
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Architecture Definition Document (ADD) is first created as a draft output in Phase B (Business Architecture), where the Business Architecture components are initially documented. While it continues to be updated through Phases C and D, its initial creation occurs in Phase B. Phase A produces the Architecture Vision, not the ADD. Phases C and D update the ADD with Information Systems and Technology Architecture content respectively.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** d) Develop a high-level aspirational vision of the capabilities and business value to be delivered
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Developing a high-level aspirational vision is an objective of Phase A (Architecture Vision), not the Preliminary Phase. The Preliminary Phase focuses on establishing the architecture capability, including determining the Architecture Framework to use, defining Architecture Principles, and setting up the governance structure and organizational model. Options a), b), and c) are all legitimate Preliminary Phase objectives. The tricky part is that "aspirational vision" sounds like it could belong to setup, but TOGAF specifically assigns this to Phase A.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** a) Develop Baseline Business Architecture Description
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** In the Phase B step sequence, after selecting relevant architecture viewpoints, the next step is to develop the Baseline Business Architecture Description using those viewpoints. Gap analysis (option b) comes later, after both baseline and target have been described. Defining candidate roadmap components (option c) and resolving impacts (option d) occur toward the end of Phase B. The logical flow is: select viewpoints → describe baseline → describe target → perform gap analysis.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** b) Phase A: Architecture Vision
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Statement of Architecture Work is a key output of Phase A (Architecture Vision). It defines the scope, approach, and schedule for the architecture development effort and is effectively the "contract" between the architecture team and sponsors to proceed with the architecture work. It is not produced in the Preliminary Phase (which produces the framework and principles), nor in Phase B. Requirements Management is a continuous process, not a deliverable-producing phase in this context.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** d) Change Requests for existing business programs and projects
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Change Requests for existing business programs and projects are a specific input to Phase E (Opportunities & Solutions) as this phase deals with identifying implementation opportunities and addressing how the Target Architecture can be realized, including changes to existing programs. The Architecture Definition Document (option a), Architecture Requirements Specification (option b), and Capability Assessments (option c) are inputs shared across multiple phases including Phase D. Phase E uniquely considers how to consolidate change activity across the enterprise.

---

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** b) Phase E: Opportunities & Solutions
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Transition Architectures are formally defined in Phase E (Opportunities & Solutions). This phase identifies the major implementation projects and groups them into Transition Architectures that deliver incremental business value. While Phase F (Migration Planning) further refines the migration approach, the actual definition of Transition Architectures occurs in Phase E. Phase D focuses on Technology Architecture development, and Phase G deals with implementation governance of already-planned work.

---

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** a) Establish the architecture project → Identify stakeholders, concerns and business requirements → Confirm business goals → Develop Architecture Vision
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The correct sequence in Phase A begins with establishing the architecture project (including confirming management framework interactions and securing sponsorship), then identifying stakeholders, their concerns, and business requirements. After that, business goals and drivers are confirmed, and finally the Architecture Vision is developed. Option b) incorrectly places stakeholder identification before project establishment. Options c) and d) rearrange the sequence incorrectly. The logic is that you must first establish the project before you can properly identify and engage stakeholders.

---

### Question 8
**Correct Answer:** b) Phases B through F
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Architecture Roadmap is progressively developed starting in Phase B, where initial candidate roadmap components are identified from the Business Architecture. It continues to be refined through Phases C and D as more architecture content is developed, and is further elaborated in Phase E (where Transition Architectures inform it) and finalized in Phase F (Migration Planning). Phase A produces the Architecture Vision but not the Roadmap. The Roadmap is a key planning artifact that grows through the architecture development and planning phases.

---

### Question 9
**Correct Answer:** b) Compliance Assessments
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** Compliance Assessments are uniquely produced in Phase G (Implementation Governance). This phase monitors implementation projects to ensure they conform to the defined architecture, and Compliance Assessments document the results of architecture compliance reviews. Architecture Contracts (option a) are also produced in Phase G but are initially referenced in Phase E/F. Change Requests (option c) can be produced in multiple phases. "Implementation Governance Model" (option d) is not a standard TOGAF deliverable name — the correct term relates to the governance framework established earlier.

---

### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** b) Phase B: Business Architecture
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Architecture Requirements Specification is first created as a draft in Phase B (Business Architecture), alongside the Architecture Definition Document. As the ADM progresses through Phases C and D, the specification is updated with additional requirements from the Information Systems and Technology Architecture domains. Phase A focuses on producing the Architecture Vision and Statement of Architecture Work, not the detailed requirements specification. Phase E uses the completed specification as an input rather than creating it.

### Question 11

A stakeholder requesting additional viewpoints for existing architecture is not a trigger for a new ADM cycle — it would be handled as part of ongoing stakeholder management or by revisiting existing architecture documentation. Phase H triggers are about substantive changes that require architecture updates, not about additional perspectives on existing work.

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** b) It is an input to Phase A and triggers the creation of the Architecture Vision

The Preliminary Phase is triggered by organizational needs to establish an architecture capability, not by a Request for Architecture Work. The Request specifies the business reasons for the architecture work and is the formal mechanism to commission architecture development.

This flexibility allows organizations to prioritize based on their specific needs and the nature of the architecture engagement.
