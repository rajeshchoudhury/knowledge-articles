## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer: c)**
ABBs capture architecture requirements at a technology-independent level while SBBs represent actual components that will be used to implement the architecture. ABBs are conceptual and define what is needed; SBBs are physical/logical and define how it will be built.

### Question 2
**Correct Answer: a)**
A viewpoint is the perspective (the position or angle) from which a system is observed. A view is the actual representation or picture of the system as seen from that viewpoint. The viewpoint is reusable; the view is specific to a particular architecture.

### Question 3
**Correct Answer: b)**
Deliverables are contractually specified work products that are formally reviewed, agreed upon, and signed off. Artifacts are more granular work products (catalogs, matrices, diagrams) that describe the architecture and are typically contained within deliverables.

### Question 4
**Correct Answer: c)**
Skipping Phase B would deprive subsequent phases of the essential business context. While TOGAF allows ADM tailoring, the business architecture provides the foundation for all other architecture domains. Without it, information systems may not align with business needs, goals, and objectives.

### Question 5
**Correct Answer: b)**
Architecture Contracts in general can exist at multiple points in the ADM, covering agreements between various parties. In Phase G specifically, contracts govern implementation projects to ensure they conform to the defined architecture. Phase G contracts are about monitoring and enforcing compliance during project delivery.

### Question 6
**Correct Answer: a)**
The Preliminary Phase establishes the architecture capability—defining principles, framework, tools, and governance. Phase A begins a specific architecture project by defining the scope, constraints, stakeholders, and creating a high-level vision of where the organization needs to go.

### Question 7
**Correct Answer: b)**
The Architecture Landscape is the view of architectural assets across the enterprise (Strategic, Segment, Capability levels). The Architecture Repository is the facility that stores all outputs: reference materials, standards, governance logs, and the landscape content itself.

### Question 8
**Correct Answer: b)**
TOGAF's Requirements Management process operates continuously and is the mechanism for handling newly discovered requirements or gaps. If a significant gap is found in Phase E, the ADM iteration process allows returning to the appropriate earlier phase to address it.

### Question 9
**Correct Answer: a)**
A Transition Architecture represents a formally defined intermediate state between the baseline and target architectures. The Target Architecture is the desired end state. An organization may need multiple Transition Architectures to incrementally reach the Target Architecture.

### Question 10
**Correct Answer: a)**
The Enterprise Continuum provides a classification taxonomy (from generic to specific) for classifying architectural and solution assets. The Architecture Repository is where all these classified assets, along with other outputs, are actually stored and managed.

### Question 11
**Correct Answer: b)**
The Architecture Continuum classifies reusable architecture descriptions from Foundation through Common Systems to Industry to Organization-Specific. The Solutions Continuum mirrors this but for solution implementations. Both range from generic to specific.

### Question 12
**Correct Answer: b)**
Non-conformance doesn't automatically halt a project. The finding is documented, and the governance process determines appropriate action—which may include granting a dispensation (time-limited exception), requiring architecture changes, or other remedial actions.

### Question 13
**Correct Answer: a)**
Architecture Principles are broad, enduring rules (e.g., "Maximize Reuse") that guide architecture decisions. Architecture Requirements are specific, testable conditions (e.g., "System must support 10,000 concurrent users") that the architecture must satisfy.

### Question 14
**Correct Answer: b)**
Requirements Management is a continuous process that operates throughout all ADM phases. Phase H specifically manages post-implementation changes to the architecture. Both coexist—Phase H may generate new requirements that are managed by the Requirements Management process.

### Question 15
**Correct Answer: a)**
Phase E identifies work packages, groups them into Transition Architectures, and evaluates opportunities. Without this, Phase F's migration planning would lack the essential inputs of defined work packages and transition architectures to sequence and plan.

### Question 16
**Correct Answer: a)**
Catalogs provide lists of building blocks (e.g., Technology Standards Catalog). Matrices show relationships between building blocks (e.g., Application/Data Matrix). Diagrams provide visual depictions (e.g., Network Diagram). This is a fundamental classification in the TOGAF Content Framework.

### Question 17
**Correct Answer: a)**
Architecture Governance is the broader practice encompassing policies, processes, procedures, and oversight. The Architecture Board is the organizational structure (committee/body) responsible for executing and overseeing the governance activities.

### Question 18
**Correct Answer: b)**
When conflicts arise between phases, TOGAF's Requirements Management process is the appropriate mechanism for managing the change. This may involve revisiting the earlier phase to resolve the conflict rather than proceeding with inconsistencies.

### Question 19
**Correct Answer: b)**
Phase A is where stakeholders are initially identified and engagement approaches are established. In subsequent phases, stakeholder management shifts to ongoing communication, addressing specific domain concerns, and ensuring continued buy-in as the architecture develops in detail.

### Question 20
**Correct Answer: a)**
Gap analysis is a technique applied in context. In Phase B, it compares baseline and target business architectures to identify business gaps. In Phase E, gap analysis is used at a higher level to evaluate the overall solution space and identify work packages needed to close gaps across all domains.

### Question 21
**Correct Answer: a)**
The Content Framework provides the metamodel and classification for architectural work products. The Architecture Repository provides the storage and retrieval facility. The Content Framework defines "what goes in," and the Repository is "where it's stored."

### Question 22
**Correct Answer: b)**
Unaddressed concerns should never be silently discarded. They should be documented and handled through appropriate mechanisms—creating requirements, initiating new architecture work, or escalating through the governance process for decision-making.

### Question 23
**Correct Answer: a)**
Business Scenarios are a technique used in Phase A to derive architecture requirements from business problems. They describe a situation, identify stakeholders, and articulate the business and technical environment. Use Cases are more focused on functional system interactions.

### Question 24
**Correct Answer: a)**
In TOGAF, compliance refers to adherence to governance processes and standards. Conformance refers to how well an implementation aligns with the defined architecture. While often used together, they address different aspects of architectural alignment. Note: TOGAF uses these terms carefully though they are sometimes conflated.

### Question 25
**Correct Answer: b)**
TOGAF explicitly states that the ADM can and should be tailored to the organization's needs. This includes combining phases, provided the essential outputs and activities are still addressed. The ADM is designed to be adapted, not followed rigidly.

### Question 26
**Correct Answer: a)**
On the Enterprise Continuum, Reference Models (like the TRM) sit toward the generic end, providing abstract, technology-neutral taxonomies. Organization-Specific Architectures sit at the specific end, tailored to the organization's particular needs and technology choices.

### Question 27
**Correct Answer: b)**
Phase A's Architecture Vision is a key decision point. If stakeholders reject the vision, the response should be to understand their concerns, re-scope or revise the vision, or determine if the project should proceed at all. The Statement of Architecture Work requires stakeholder sign-off.

### Question 28
**Correct Answer: a)**
Phases (Preliminary, A–H) are the logical steps within one ADM cycle. Iterations describe the process of repeating phases—either within a cycle (going back from Phase D to Phase B) or across cycles (starting a new ADM cycle for the next iteration of the architecture).

### Question 29
**Correct Answer: a)**
The Architecture Vision describes the target state, business value, and high-level view of what the architecture will achieve. The Statement of Architecture Work is a project-governance document that defines scope, resources, schedule, constraints, and approach for the architecture project.

### Question 30
**Correct Answer: a)**
Architecture Partitioning divides the enterprise architecture into manageable areas (e.g., by business unit, domain, or geography) with clear ownership. Segmentation breaks down an architecture landscape into time-sequenced segments that can be individually developed.

### Question 31
**Correct Answer: b)**
TOGAF's ADM is designed for iterative development. When significant issues in earlier phases are discovered, the appropriate response is to iterate back to that phase using the Requirements Management process, rather than proceeding with a known-flawed foundation.

### Question 32
**Correct Answer: a)**
An Architecture Framework (like TOGAF itself) provides the overall structure, tools, vocabulary, and reference models for architecture. An Architecture Method (like the ADM) provides the specific step-by-step process for developing architecture within that framework.

### Question 33
**Correct Answer: a)**
Constraints are fixed limitations imposed externally (e.g., regulatory requirements, budget, existing contracts) that must be adhered to. Principles are voluntarily adopted guidelines that shape decisions but can have managed exceptions when justified.

### Question 34
**Correct Answer: a)**
The Architecture Roadmap (built incrementally through Phases B–E) is a high-level schedule of work packages and Transition Architectures. The Implementation and Migration Plan (Phase F output) provides detailed, project-level planning for executing the roadmap.

### Question 35
**Correct Answer: b)**
Phase G governs implementation against the migration plan from Phase F. Without agreed work packages, Transition Architectures, and sequenced implementation projects from Phase F, Phase G would have no defined baseline against which to govern implementation conformance.

### Question 36
**Correct Answer: a)**
Foundation Architectures (like the TRM) provide the most generic architectural guidance applicable across all enterprises. Common Systems Architectures (like the III-RM) add specificity by addressing shared services and components common across many organizations. This progression continues to Industry and Organization-Specific levels.

### Question 37
**Correct Answer: b)**
Interoperability concerns the ability of different systems to meaningfully exchange and use information. Integration is about the technical mechanisms for connecting systems. Systems can be integrated without being truly interoperable if they cannot meaningfully use the exchanged data.

### Question 38
**Correct Answer: a)**
Patterns provide reusable solution templates that describe how to solve recurring architectural problems. Building Blocks are reusable components (either ABBs or SBBs) that represent specific capabilities. A pattern might specify how building blocks should be combined.

### Question 39
**Correct Answer: b)**
TOGAF accommodates organizations without formal baselines. Phase B should proceed by documenting the current state as understood (even if informal) while defining the target business architecture. The gap analysis then identifies what needs to change from the current to the target state.

### Question 40
**Correct Answer: b)**
TOGAF's ADM risk management focuses specifically on risks related to the architecture and its transformation—risks of wrong architectural decisions, technology obsolescence, etc. Traditional project risk management addresses schedule, budget, resource, and other project delivery concerns.
