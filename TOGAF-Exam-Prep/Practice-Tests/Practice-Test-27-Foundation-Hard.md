## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** c) Technology Standards Catalog
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Technology Standards Catalog is classified as a catalog in the Technology Architecture domain. It lists technology standards and their approval status. The Application Communication Diagram (option a) is a diagram. The Business Interaction Matrix (option b) is a matrix showing interactions between organizations/actors. The Application/Data Matrix (option d) is a matrix showing relationships between applications and data entities. Understanding the artifact classification (catalog vs. matrix vs. diagram) is essential for the TOGAF Content Framework.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** b) An artifact is a work product that is contained within a deliverable and represents an architectural aspect
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** In the TOGAF Content Framework, artifacts are granular work products (catalogs, matrices, diagrams) that describe architecture from a specific viewpoint. Deliverables are formal, contractually specified work products that are reviewed, agreed upon, and signed off. A deliverable contains one or more artifacts. Option a) reverses the containment relationship. Option c) incorrectly equates them. Option d) reverses the hierarchy. The key distinction is that deliverables are the contractual outputs while artifacts are the architectural content within them.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** c) Constraint
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The core content metamodel includes entities such as Actor, Role, Business Service, Data Entity, Application Component, Technology Component, and others that form the fundamental architecture description. Constraint belongs to the Governance Extension of the metamodel, not the core content. Data Entity (option a), Technology Component (option b), and Business Service (option d) are all part of the core content metamodel. Constraints, along with Principles and Assumptions, are governance-related entities that extend the core model.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** a) Application/Function Matrix
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Application/Function Matrix is the artifact that shows the relationship between applications and the business functions they support. It is a matrix in the Information Systems Architecture domain that maps application components to business functions. The Application Portfolio Catalog (option b) is a catalog listing applications and their attributes. The Application Communication Diagram (option c) shows communication between applications. The Application/Organization Matrix (option d) maps applications to organizational units. The precise naming of artifacts and their purpose is critical TOGAF knowledge.

---

### Question 5
**Correct Answer:** b) A component that captures architecture requirements and directs the implementation of a Solution Building Block
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** An ABB captures architecture requirements and directs and guides the implementation of SBBs. ABBs are defined at an architecture level and specify required capabilities without prescribing a specific implementation. Option a) describes building blocks in general, not specifically ABBs. Option c) describes SBBs, which are vendor-specific implementations. Option d) describes physical technology components. The key characteristic of an ABB is that it specifies WHAT is needed (requirements and interfaces) while an SBB specifies HOW it is implemented.

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** b) It is a Business Architecture catalog
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The Organization/Actor Catalog is classified as a catalog in the Business Architecture domain. It captures the organizations, organizational units, and actors involved in the business. Catalogs list instances of a single entity type, which is exactly what this artifact does — listing organizational entities. It is not a matrix (which shows relationships between two entity types) or a diagram (which provides visual representation). It belongs to the Business Architecture domain, not Information Systems Architecture.

### Question 7
**Correct Answer:** b) Business Service
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** In the Content Metamodel, a Business Service represents a unit of functionality that is meaningful to an end user or consumer. It supports business capabilities and is exposed through defined interfaces. A Business Function (option a) represents an internal unit of business capability that is performed continuously within an organization — it is internally focused, not user-facing. An Application Component (option c) is an IT concept, not a business concept. An Information System Service (option d) is the IS-level service that may realize a Business Service. The distinction between Function (internal) and Service (externally visible) is critical.

The ADD contains various artifacts from Phases B, C, and D. It is not itself an artifact (option a), which would be a single catalog, matrix, or diagram. It is not a building block (option c) or a catalog (option d). Understanding the deliverable vs. artifact distinction is fundamental to the Content Framework.
