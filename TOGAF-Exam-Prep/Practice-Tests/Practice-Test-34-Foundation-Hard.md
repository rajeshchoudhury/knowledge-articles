## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** b) A Viewpoint establishes the conventions, languages, and notations for constructing a particular type of View; a View is the representation of an architecture from the perspective of a specific set of concerns using that Viewpoint  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 — View vs. Viewpoint  
**Explanation:** This is the foundational distinction in ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 and TOGAF. A Viewpoint is like a template or specification — it defines the model kinds, languages, notations, and conventions. A View is the actual work product — an instance created using that Viewpoint to represent a specific architecture for specific stakeholders. Option (a) reverses the definitions. Option (c) incorrectly treats them as interchangeable. Option (d) incorrectly separates them by stakeholder vs. concern when both are related to both.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** b) Application/Technology Matrix Viewpoint — mapping application components to their hosting technology platforms and infrastructure  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** Viewpoint Selection Criteria  
**Explanation:** IT Operations staff need to understand where applications are deployed and what technology infrastructure supports them. The Application/Technology Matrix Viewpoint directly addresses this by mapping application components to technology platforms. This enables operations to plan capacity, manage infrastructure dependencies, and support incident management. The Business Process Viewpoint (a) would not provide deployment information. The Business Service/Information Viewpoint (c) focuses on information exchange, not deployment. The Stakeholder Map Viewpoint (d) is about stakeholder relationships, not technology allocation.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** c) Non-repudiation  
**Difficulty:** Hard  
**Topic:** Security Services Taxonomy  
**Explanation:** Non-repudiation is the security service that ensures a party cannot deny participation in a transaction. It provides irrefutable evidence of actions taken, typically through digital signatures, timestamps, and audit trails. Audit (a) records events for review but does not inherently prevent denial. Integrity (b) ensures data has not been altered, which is related but distinct. Authentication (d) verifies identity but does not prevent subsequent denial of participation.

---

### Question 4
**Correct Answer:** b) Develop separate architecture views using appropriate viewpoints tailored to each stakeholder's specific concerns, while ensuring traceability between the strategic and program-level perspectives  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** Stakeholder-to-Concern Mapping  
**Explanation:** TOGAF recommends addressing different stakeholder concerns through appropriately tailored views. The CIO needs a strategic portfolio view addressing long-term technology direction, while the Program Manager needs a delivery-focused view with timelines and dependencies. Creating separate views ensures each audience receives relevant information in an appropriate format. Traceability between the views ensures the program-level work supports the strategic direction. A single combined view (a) would not serve either audience well. Prioritizing by authority (c) ignores legitimate concerns. Delegation to the Architecture Board (d) abdicates the architect's stakeholder management responsibility.

Option (c) limits it to Phase A, when architecture descriptions evolve throughout the ADM. Option (d) confuses Architecture Description with Architecture Contract.

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** b) Keep satisfied — provide enough information to maintain their support without overwhelming them, and watch for any increase in their level of interest  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** Stakeholder Power/Interest Grid  
**Explanation:** The Power/Interest grid defines four quadrants with distinct management strategies. HIGH power / LOW interest stakeholders must be "kept satisfied" — their power means they can significantly impact the architecture effort, but their low interest means they don't want to be burdened with details. The key is providing enough attention to maintain their support and being alert if their interest increases (which would move them to the "manage closely" quadrant). Option (a) is for LOW power / LOW interest. Option (c) is for HIGH power / HIGH interest. Option (d) is for LOW power / HIGH interest.

### Question 9

**Explanation:** The RACI matrix is a governance tool that clarifies roles and responsibilities. R (Responsible) identifies who does the work. ### Question 10
**Correct Answer:** a) Authentication, Authorization, and Audit  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** Security Services Taxonomy  
**Explanation:** Authentication (verifying identity), Authorization (controlling what authenticated users can do), and Audit (tracking what users actually did) form the classic "AAA" triad for access control. Together they ensure that only verified users can access resources, that access is limited to appropriate permissions, and that all access is logged for accountability. Confidentiality (b) is about protecting data from unauthorized disclosure. Non-repudiation (c, d) is about preventing transaction denial. While related to security, the CIA triad (b) addresses data protection properties rather than access control specifically.

The Physical Layer (a) aligns more with Technology Architecture. The Component Layer (c) aligns with solution-level implementation. The Logical Layer (d) aligns more closely with the Data and Application Architecture phases where detailed security policies are specified.

### Question 12

Business Architecture addresses security policies, roles, and organizational controls. Data Architecture addresses data classification, privacy, and information security. Application Architecture addresses application-level security controls, authentication, and authorization. Technology Architecture addresses infrastructure security, network security, and platform hardening. Confining security to any single domain (a, c) creates gaps. Security requires specific artifacts beyond just principles (d).

### Question 14
**Correct Answer:** b) From the earliest phases (Preliminary and Phase A) through all subsequent phases, ensuring privacy requirements are treated as first-class architectural concerns  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** Privacy by Design in EA  
**Explanation:** Privacy by Design (PbD), as articulated by Ann Cavoukian and now embedded in regulations like GDPR, requires that privacy is proactively embedded into the design of systems and processes from the outset — not bolted on afterward. In TOGAF terms, this means privacy must be addressed starting from Architecture Principles (Preliminary Phase), through the Architecture Vision (Phase A), and into every domain architecture phase. Limiting privacy to change management (a), data architecture (c), or requirements management alone (d) violates the fundamental PbD principle of being proactive and preventive.

**Explanation:** While interoperability affects many stakeholders, Solution Architects and Application Developers are MOST directly concerned because they are responsible for designing and implementing the integration points, defining interface contracts, selecting integration patterns (APIs, messaging, etc.), and maintaining data exchange specifications. End Users (a) experience the effect but don't manage the cause. IT Operations (b) monitors integration but doesn't design it. The CFO (d) may care about cost implications but interoperability is not their primary concern domain.

### Question 16
**Correct Answer:** b) The Chief Architect is the single individual who ultimately answers for the correct and thorough completion of the Architecture Contract and has the authority to approve or reject the final deliverable  
**Difficulty:** Expert  
**Topic:** RACI Matrix Usage  
**Explanation:** In a RACI matrix, "Accountable" (sometimes called "Approver") designates the single person who ultimately owns the deliverable. There must be exactly one "A" per activity. The Accountable person doesn't necessarily do the work (that's "Responsible"), but they ensure it is completed correctly and have sign-off authority. Option (a) describes "Responsible." Option (c) describes "Informed." Option (d) describes "Consulted." The distinction between A and R is one of the most commonly confused RACI concepts.
