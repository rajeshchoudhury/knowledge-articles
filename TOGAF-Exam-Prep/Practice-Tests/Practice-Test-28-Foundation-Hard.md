## Answer Key

### Question 1
**Correct Answer:** b) 6 levels; the non-compliant level is called "Non-Conformant"
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** TOGAF defines exactly 6 levels of architecture compliance: Irrelevant, Consistent, Compliant, Conformant, Compatible, and Non-Conformant. The "Non-Conformant" level is used when an implementation does not conform to the architecture specification and has no dispensation. Option a) and d) incorrectly state 5 levels. Option c) incorrectly identifies "Irrelevant" as the non-compliant level — Irrelevant means the specification simply doesn't apply.

---

### Question 2
**Correct Answer:** a) Conformant, Compliant, Consistent, Compatible, Non-Conformant, Irrelevant
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** The six TOGAF compliance levels, from most to least compliant, are: Irrelevant (specification doesn't apply), Consistent (no contradictions but not complete coverage), Compliant (agrees with specification, may have extras), Conformant (fully satisfies specification), Compatible (doesn't conflict but incomplete), and Non-Conformant (doesn't meet specification). However, the ordering presented in option a) — Conformant (most fully compliant), Compliant, Consistent, Compatible, Non-Conformant, Irrelevant — represents the typical ordering from most aligned to least. Note: TOGAF doesn't specify a strict hierarchy, but option a) best represents a logical ordering.

---

### Question 3
**Correct Answer:** c) The implementation has no features in common with the architecture specification but also does not contradict it
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** "Consistent" in TOGAF compliance means that the implementation does not conflict with the architecture specification but does not necessarily cover it either. There are no contradictions, but there may be no overlap. Option a) describes "Compatible" which has some common features. Option b) describes "Conformant" which is fully in agreement. Option d) would be closer to "Irrelevant." The key distinction for "Consistent" is the absence of contradiction without necessarily having agreement.

It is compliant in the sense that it meets the spec but isn't limited to it. Option a) describes "Conformant" (fully satisfies all requirements). Option c) is "Non-Conformant" territory. Option d) is closer to "Compatible." The distinction between Compliant (agrees + may have extras) and Conformant (fully satisfies all requirements) is a frequently tested nuance.

### Question 5

The Board provides the basis for decision-making (option a), ensures consistency (option c), and resolves conflicts (option d). The Board's role is governance and oversight, not operational management of projects. Implementation project management remains with the designated project managers, while the Board ensures architectural compliance through reviews and guidance.

### Question 6
**Correct Answer:** b) A formal agreement that acknowledges non-compliance and sets out a plan and timeline for achieving compliance
**Difficulty:** Hard
**Explanation:** A dispensation in TOGAF is a formal mechanism that acknowledges that an implementation is not currently compliant with the architecture but provides an agreed-upon plan and timeline for achieving compliance. It is NOT a permanent exemption (option a) — it is temporary with a remediation path. Option c) describes a technology portfolio decision, not a dispensation. Option d) describes a rejection, not a dispensation. Dispensations allow projects to proceed while maintaining governance oversight and accountability for eventual compliance.

### Question 7

Options b), c), and d) present the steps in illogical orders. The preparation steps (identify people, scope, checklists) must precede the execution steps (schedule, carry out, report).
