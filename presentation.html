The Combined Ratio

Combined Ratio = Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio

Loss Ratio = (Incurred Losses + LAE) / Earned Premium
Expense Ratio = Underwriting Expenses / Written Premium

Below 100% = Underwriting profit  |  Claims directly drives the Loss Ratio
Speaker Notes
Claims is the fulfillment arm of the insurance promise. When a policyholder experiences a covered loss, the claims organization investigates, evaluates, and settles the claim. Emphasize that every dollar saved in claims flows directly to the bottom line.

Lines of Business (LOBs)

Collision, Comprehensive, BI/PD Liability, UM/UIM, PIP, Rental. High volume, high automation potential, telematics data.

Dwelling, Contents, Loss of Use, Liability. Weather-driven CAT surges, contractor management, mold complexity.

Straight-Through Processing (STP)

Claims filed, adjusted, and paid without human intervention

STP Eligibility Criteria

  • Policy active and in force on date of loss
  • Coverage confirmed for reported loss type
  • No injuries reported (PD-only)
  • Single vehicle / single peril
  • Estimated severity below STP threshold
  • Fraud score < 300 (low risk)
  • No prior open claim on same policy
  • No attorney representation
  • All required FNOL fields populated

Coverage Verification

Is this loss covered under the policy?

Retrieve Policy from PAS
→
Policy In Force?
→
Coverage Applies?
→
Exclusions?
→
Decision

✅ Covered

All coverage confirmed. Proceed with claim handling. Create Exposure(s) per coverage + claimant.

❓ Coverage Question

Gray area — refer to coverage counsel for opinion. May take days/weeks to resolve.

⚠️ Reservation of Rights (ROR)

Uncertainty exists. ROR letter sent to insured. Investigation continues under reservation.

❌ Denied

Not covered under policy. Written denial with specific policy language cited. Claimant may appeal or file suit.

Speaker Notes
Coverage verification is a critical gate. Emphasize the difference between duty to defend (broader, triggered by allegations) and duty to indemnify (narrower, based on facts). ROR letters preserve the carrier's right to later deny while still investigating.

Actuarial Methods

Chain-Ladder (Development)

Historical loss development patterns project ultimate losses. Best for mature LOBs.

Bornhuetter-Ferguson

Blends expected loss ratio with actual emergence. Less volatile for immature years.

Frequency × Severity

Projects claim counts and average costs separately. Useful when trends diverge.

ML Predicted Reserve Emerging

Individual claim-level predictions using gradient boosting on FNOL data. Output: P10/P50/P90.

Key Decision: Repair vs. Total Loss

Threshold: Repair cost ≥ 70–80% of ACV (varies by state)

If Repairable:
DRP shop (pre-negotiated rates) or customer-choice shop
Payment = Repair cost − Deductible

If Total Loss:
ACV via comparable analysis (CCC Valuescope)
Settlement = ACV + taxes/fees − Deductible
Salvage sold via Copart / IAA auction

Supplement Handling

  • Shop discovers hidden damage during teardown
  • Submits revised estimate + photos electronically
  • Adjuster reviews and approves/denies
  • Re-check total loss threshold with new amount
  • Supplement rate: 15–25% of repairable claims

DRP Benefits

  • Pre-negotiated labor rates & parts agreements
  • Guaranteed repairs for policyholder
  • Direct electronic assignment (CIECA/EMS)
  • Faster cycle time (avg 3–5 days faster)

Disability Types

TTD — Temporary Total

Cannot work at all. 2/3 AWW, state max cap. Until RTW or MMI.

TPD — Temporary Partial

Can work, reduced capacity. 2/3 × (pre-injury − current earnings).

PPD — Permanent Partial

Permanent impairment but can work. Rated by % impairment. Scheduled or unscheduled.

PTD — Permanent Total

Cannot return to any work. Lifetime benefits in most states.

Medical Management

  • Bill Review: Fee schedule compliance, UCR rates, duplicate billing, network repricing. Savings: 30–50%.
  • Utilization Review: Pre-auth for surgery, MRI, specialist, DME. Evidence-based guidelines (ODG/ACOEM).
  • Nurse Case Management: Coordinate care, monitor treatment, facilitate RTW.
  • Rx Management: Formulary compliance, opioid monitoring, PDMP checks, taper protocols.

Payment & Settlement

Payment Validation Checks

1. Authority Check

Payment ≤ adjuster's authority? If no → supervisor approval.

2. Reserve Sufficiency

Payment ≤ available reserve? If no → increase reserve first.

3. Duplicate Detection

Same payee + amount + date exists? Flag for review.

4. OFAC Screening

Payee against Treasury sanctions list. Hit → compliance hold.

5. Litigation Hold

In suit? Requires litigation manager sign-off.

6. Medicare Query

BI settlement? Check Section 111 before disbursement.

Catastrophe (CAT) Operations

CAT Timeline

T-72h: Monitor

Weather monitoring, geo-fence policies, estimate exposure.

T-48h: Activate

CAT team activated, IA firms contracted, vendors staged.

T-24h: Outreach

Proactive SMS/email to policyholders. IVR updated.

T+0: Event

Landfall / occurrence. FNOL surge begins.

T+1-3d: Surge

5,000–50,000+ claims/day. Chatbot FNOL, overflow call center.

T+3-90d: Sustain

200–2,000 field adjusters deployed. Virtual triage for <$10K claims.

T+90d+: Closeout

Target 80% closed by day 90. Manage supplements, depreciation, subro.

CAT Challenges

  • Volume Surge: 10x–100x normal. Elastic cloud scaling critical.
  • Independent Adjusters: Crawford, Sedgwick, Engle Martin contracted for field capacity.
  • Demand Surge: Material/labor costs spike 20–40% post-CAT.
  • Public Adjusters: PAs hired by policyholders take 10–20% of settlement.
  • Advance Payments: Emergency ALE $2K–$5K within 48 hours.
  • Anti-Concurrent Causation: Wind vs. flood coverage disputes.
  • FEMA / NFIP Coordination: Federal flood claims handled under separate rules.
